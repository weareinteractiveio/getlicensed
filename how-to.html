Important information

It is your responsibility to ensure that you have satisfied the training requirements - you will not be granted a Licence unless you do so.

The following SIA training activities are licensable:

  • Cash and Valuable in Transit
  • Close Protection
  • Door Supervision
  • Public Space Surveillance (CCTV)
  • Security Guard
  • Vehicle Immobilisation

It normally takes around six weeks for each application to be processed; this time starts from when a successfully completed SIA Licence application form is received (when the SIA receives a correctly-completed application, they will write to the candidate to confirm this).

It usually takes one to two working days for a correctly completed application to be checked and scanned into the SIA's system. Due to the high volume of applications received, this process can take approximately five working days. Complex applications, for example, applications subject to the Criminal Records Bureau, qualification enquiries and those who require overseas criminality checks, may take longer than six weeks to process.

A decision will then be reached on whether to issue a Licence. The SIA will write explaining their decision and, if successfully granted, a Licence will be issued to cover you for a period of three years.

WARNING: It is a legal requirement for anyone working in the United Kingdom Security Industry to have the necessary training and qualifications, accredited by the Security Industry Authority. To practice in the industry without a valid SIA Licence is a criminal offence, which means you could be fined and arrested.

The SIA takes allegations of training malpractice very seriously and will promptly take action to suspend Licences after receiving information from any awarding body. Training is an essential part of the licensing process; ensuring that all those working in the Security Industry are properly equipped to carry out their role. Any person found to have been complicit in malpractice will have their Licence revoked. If you suspect any malpractice with your training provider, please report it to us immediately.

SIA Licence withdrawal or suspension

The SIA can withdraw or suspend your Licence at any time if you fail to meet the licensing requirements.

Applying for an SIA licence application

New SIA Licence Applications

If you are applying for an SIA Licence for the first time, please use the form on SIA website. When the form is submitted, you can take your identity documents to one of 750 Post Offices in the UK to complete your application.

The post office will:
  • Check your application
  • Verify and return your documents
  • Capture a digital photograph with an electronic signature
  • Process the payment
  • The post office will forward the verified application to the SIA to complete
Renewing your SIA Licence

If you are renewing your SIA Licence please use the SIA's telephone service.
